About


I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Computer Science at University of Birmingham, and a Visiting Researcher at the University of Oxford. Before joining Birmingham, I was fortunate to work as a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Biomedical Image Analysis (BioMedIA) group and the Visual Geometry Group (VGG) at the University of Oxford, advised by Prof Alison Noble and Prof Andrew Zisserman. I obtained my PhD in Computer Science from City University of Hong Kong, advised by Prof Qingxiong Yang and Prof Rynson W. H. Lau. I have been lucky to work as a visiting scholar in the Image Formation and Processing(IFP) group at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), advised by Prof Thomas S. Huang. I was also lucky to work with Dr Linchao Bao as an intern at Tencent AI Lab. My research interests include Computer Vision, Machine Learning and Medical Imaging. I am particularly interested in learning better representations by exploring auxiliary from different modalities and reducing the dependency on supervisions, with application to vision and healthcare problems.

I am looking for self-motivated PhD students (as well as PostDoc, Master students, RAs, Visiting students/researchers, etc.). Please drop me an email if you are interested in working with me!
